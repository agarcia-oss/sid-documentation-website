A loyalty program is one of the tools used by the loyalty process to retain customers. For example, another tool is the ProductOffering Commitment.

An example of loyalty program could be « Change Your Mobile Offer »:
• available only for prepaid contracts, therefore incompatible with postpaid contracts,
• month after month the customer earns points and depending on the number of points he has, he can buy a new handset at a lower price using his points.
Another example could be a “more time for calls” program that gives you more time for calls according to your seniority (i.e.: 5% after six months of seniority, 10% after two years of seniority, etc.).
And these two loyalty programs could have a cost and be incompatible.
The aim of marketing is to find levers in the form of rewards to enhance customer loyalty to its operator.
Most of the time, the conditions to reward a customer are based on
• seniority in customer relationship,
• the importance of revenue from the customer,
• or the risk of churn.
The more the customer remains with the operator or consumes, the more he will be rewarded.

Different types of rewards can be considered:
• physical rewards: accessories, terminals, points usable to buy accessories / terminals ...
• virtual rewards: free usage, vouchers ...
The rewards that can be bought thanks to loyalty earns correspond to ProductSpecification & ProductOffering with a price allowing a specific currency (loyalty points).
A loyalty program is a part of a contract and may be either mandatory or not (packaging rule, pre-requisite).
As a loyalty program represents a cost for the operator, the latter avoids that the customer may have several loyalty programs.
To do so, Loyalty Programs might be incompatible with each other and in this case the customer has to choose only one LoyaltyProgram.
Therefore, a loyalty program is a type of AtomicProductSpecification, is packaged through ProductOffering and is instantiated in the same manner in the installed base (LoyaltyProgramProduct that is a type of ProductComponent).
A LoyaltyProgramProdSpec defines the LoyaltyRules that have to be checked in order to identify the actions to apply. A LoyaltyRule is a type of PolicyRule.
Depending on the type of LoyaltyRules a LoyaltyAccount might be needed to collect gains or not. This is defined by the needsLoyaltyAccount attribute on the LoyaltyProgramProdSpec entity.
