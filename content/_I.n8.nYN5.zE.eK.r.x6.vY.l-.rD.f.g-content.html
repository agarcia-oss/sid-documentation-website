Class MetricDimension

A metric dimension designates a method of organizing individual metrics into common sets, related by the value of the named dimension. An example would be a metric for revenue--individual instance values of revenue could be organized in the dimension of geography (Region 1 Revenues, Region 2 Revenues, etc.), or by Product (Wireless Revenue, Wire-line Revenue, Broadband Revenue, Advertising Revenue, etc.). The characteristic term when discussing Dimension is "by", as in "Revenue, by Product" or "Revenue, by Region".
