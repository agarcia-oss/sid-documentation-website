As has been seen with other business entities contained within the Market/Sales domain there may be a number of other attributes that are important in characterizing CompetitorIntelligence. For example, the strategic alliances that have been formed by a Competitor, the coverage of a Competitor’s network, the number of potential customers reached by a Competitor’s network may be of interest. The CharacteristicSpec/Characteristic and Composite/Atomic patterns used through the Market/Sales domain are also applied here to accommodate this need.
An example of the use of the composite characteristic is the definition of a characteristic that consolidates all network-related attributes. This composite would be comprised of atomic characteristic instances representing name or location of the network associated with a MarketingCampaign, as well as instances representing the total number of potential customers that the networks support.
Notice that CompetitorIntelCharacteristic and CompetitorIntelCharacteristicValue entities can be shared by one or more instances of CompetitorIntelligence. This follows the same pattern that is used throughout the Market/Sales domain.
