[] Characteristic-Based Pricing and Charging
In the not-so-distant past factors influencing the price of a product were based on the usage of network resources and other resources, measured by such factors as amount of time, or some other unit, a resource was used or the amount of bandwidth consumed. But once the usage of other types of resources, such as content, is included, many other characteristics influence the price.
This model was originally envisioned to support content pricing and charging, but it can be used for any type of pricing and charging that is based on characteristics and their values. As will be seen it can also be used for usage-based pricing d charging also.
[] Policy versus Simplicity
When rules for pricing (and charging) were first being developed in the Information Framework (SID) many years ago, Reliance on the policy model was not total. An additional set of entities were under development to support simple rules, such as quantity-based pricing and tiered-based pricing. However, this additional set was not introduced in the released version of the SID. Rather, all rules relied on policy. Many members believe that using only policy is a bit extreme. A balance can be achieved between simplicity and complexity.
This balance was introduced into the SID in release 6.0 with the addition of an association between ProductSpecCharacteristicValue and ProductOfferingPrice. This provided a simple rule that enabled a price to be dependent on value(s) of one or more characteristics.
This balance continued in SID in release 9.0 with the arrival of a new Level 2 ABE (Aggregate Business Entity, a cohesive group of entities) named Pricing Logic Algorithm within the Product Offering Level 1 ABE. This ABE used Product Specification Characteristics to provide support for pricing that involves parameters which are multi-dimensional matrices, in which each dimension is defined by the valid values for a specific characteristic. For example, a PLA which rates events based on customer type, there is one instantiation in which customer type is either residential or commercial (and in which case the size of the dimension of the matrix is 2) and in other instantiation the customer type can be student, normal and senior citizen (in this case the size of the dimension is 3).
