Similarly, alterations made to other ProductOfferingPrice(s) incurred for a ProductOffering can take many different forms, such as
• Discount – A discount is a reduction in price. It could be a flat fee such as 10 dollars or a percentage, such as 50% off. A discount always applies to, and thus is associated with, one or more other ProdOfferPrices. So, a discount would be 10 dollars off the installation fee or 50% off the per-minute rate.
• Replacement – A price that replaces another ProductOfferingPrice (charged in lieu of another price). For example, purchasing two of a single product offering may result in a discounted price for both.
• Taxation – A tax amount is an increase in price due to a compulsory levy by an authorized jurisdiction. For ProductOfferingPrice this is a projected amount. The actual tax amount will be applied in the ProductPrice. See the dedicated section below on tax for fuller details.

A representative set of these types of allowances is shown in the figure below. The set by no means represents the complete set of allowances for a product offering. The Product Price Model provides a framework into which other types of allowances can, like charges, easily fit.
