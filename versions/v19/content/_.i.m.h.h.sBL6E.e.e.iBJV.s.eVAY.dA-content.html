A ResourceFunctionSpec specifies a function as a behaviour to transform inputs of any nature into outputs of any nature independently from the way it is provided. It is typically created by a function designer who may not have specific knowledge on realisation architecture (for example using English text explanations with diagrams, like RFC standards, or preferably a machine interpretable language).
The realisation of a ResourceFunctionSpec may be achieved by different ways; either SoftwareSpecification, PhysicalBlackBoxSpec or SoftBlackBoxSpecification.

But we also need to define at a ProductSpec, CFSSpec and RFSSpec level the ResourceFunctions covered by them. It can be useful when you define a ProductSpecification you want to propose before knowing the technical solution you are going to use.
At the end, before proposing a ProductSpecification you must be sure that all the ResourceFunctionSpec requested by the ProductSpecification are covered by its technical solution (RFSSpec) thanks to its ResourceSpecification.
Note: SoftwareSpecification, PhysicalBlackBoxSpec and SoftBlackBoxSpecification are sub-classes from ResourceSpecification.
